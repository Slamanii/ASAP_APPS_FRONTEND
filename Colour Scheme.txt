# Design System Document: Logistics & Delivery Excellence



## 1. Overview & Creative North Star: "The Kinetic Noir"

This design system moves away from the sterile, utilitarian aesthetic of typical logistics apps. Instead, it adopts **"The Kinetic Noir"**—a creative North Star that treats the movement of goods as a premium, high-speed experience.
By leveraging a deep, midnight foundation (`surface`) contrasted against high-octane oranges (`primary`), we create an environment that feels both authoritative and urgent. We break the "template" look through **intentional asymmetry**, using staggered card layouts and expansive typography scales that feel more like a luxury editorial magazine than a database. The goal is to provide the user with a sense of "command and control" through sophisticated depth and breathing room.



---



## 2. Colors

Our palette is rooted in a high-contrast dark mode designed to reduce eye strain for couriers and customers alike, while maintaining a signature "glow."



### Core Palette

- **Primary (`#ff923e`):** Used for critical action paths and brand presence.
- **Surface (`#080e1c`):** The foundation. A deep, saturated navy-black.
- **On-Surface (`#e0e5f9`):** High-readability cool white for primary information.
- **On-Surface-Variant (`#a5abbd`):** The "Secondary Text" for timestamps and metadata.



### The "No-Line" Rule

To achieve a premium feel, **1px solid borders are strictly prohibited** for sectioning. Definition must be created through background shifts. For example, a delivery tracking card (`surface-container-high`) should sit atop the main background (`surface`) without a stroke. The contrast in tonal value is sufficient to define the boundary.



### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting

Use the `surface-container` tiers to create "physical" depth:

- **Surface (Base):** Global background.
- **Surface-Container-Low:** Subtle grouping areas (e.g., background behind a list).
- **Surface-Container-Highest:** Interactive cards and active delivery modules.


### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule

Floating elements, such as the bottom navigation or urgent alerts, should utilize a **Glassmorphism** effect:

- **Background:** `surface-variant` at 60% opacity.
- **Effect:** 20px Backdrop Blur.
- **Signature Texture:** Apply a subtle linear gradient from `primary` to `primary-container` on major CTAs to give them a "machined" metallic sheen rather than a flat, plastic appearance.



---



## 3. Typography

We utilize a dual-font strategy to balance character with functional clarity.



* **Display & Headlines (Plus Jakarta Sans):** Chosen for its modern, geometric curves. Use `display-lg` for welcome states and `headline-sm` for section titles like "Track Shipping."
* **Body & Labels (Inter):** The industry standard for legibility. Used for all data-heavy points (Tracking IDs, addresses).



**Editorial Hierarchy:** Use extreme scale differences. A `display-md` page title ("My Orders") paired with a `label-sm` metadata tag ("#ASAP-EQSW") creates a sophisticated, intentional contrast that guides the eye immediately to the most important information.



---



## 4. Elevation & Depth

In this system, elevation is a product of light and shadow, not lines.



### The Layering Principle

Stack tiers to create natural lift. Place a `surface-container-highest` card (the most urgent delivery) on top of a `surface-container-low` section. The "lift" is perceived through the shift in brightness.



### Ambient Shadows

For floating elements like the "Link Wallet" button or the floating action menu:

- **Shadow:** 0px 12px 32px.
- **Color:** `surface-container-lowest` at 8% opacity.
- **Tint:** The shadow should be tinted with 2% of the `primary` orange to simulate the orange light reflecting off the dark surface.



### The "Ghost Border" Fallback

If a container requires a border for accessibility (e.g., input fields), use the **Ghost Border**:

- **Token:** `outline-variant`.
- **Opacity:** 15%.
- **Rule:** Never use 100% opacity for borders; it breaks the "Kinetic Noir" immersion.



---



## 5. Components



### Primary Buttons (Large)

- **Rounding:** `full` (9999px) for a "pill" aesthetic that feels ergonomic.
- **Style:** Gradient fill from `primary` to `primary-container`.
- **Text:** `title-md` (Inter, Bold).
- **Padding:** `4` (1rem) vertical, `8` (2rem) horizontal.



### Rounded Cards

- **Rounding:** `xl` (1.5rem) for main containers; `lg` (1rem) for nested items.
- **Logic:** Forbid dividers. Use `spacing-6` (1.5rem) to separate internal card content (e.g., separating "Pickup" from "Dropoff" addresses).



### Bottom Navigation

- **Structure:** Home, Deliveries, Account.
- **Visuals:** Transparent background with a `surface-variant` backdrop blur.
- **Active State:** Use the `primary` orange for the icon and a small `primary` glow dot beneath the active label.



### Input Fields

- **Background:** `surface-container-highest`.
- **Shape:** `md` (0.75rem).
- **Interaction:** On focus, the `outline` becomes `primary` at 40% opacity—a soft glow rather than a hard line.



---



## 6. Do's and Don'ts



### Do

- **Do** use `spacing-10` and `spacing-12` between major sections to allow the dark theme to "breathe."
- **Do** use the `tertiary` (yellow/gold) tokens for "In Progress" or "Pending" states to maintain high visibility against the blue-gray background.
- **Do** utilize `surface-bright` for hover states on dark cards to provide immediate tactile feedback.



### Don't

- **Don't** use pure white (#FFFFFF) for text. Always use `on-surface` (#e0e5f9) to prevent "haloing" and visual vibration.
- **Don't** use standard 1px dividers to separate delivery items in a list. Use a `2px` vertical gap of the base `surface` color to create a "slotted" look.
- **Don't** use shadows on every card. Shadows are reserved for elements that actually "float" over the UI, not for those that are part of the page flow.
